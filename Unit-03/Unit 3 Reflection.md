# Unit 3 Reflection

In Unit 3, I worked on a client project whereby I designed and developed a webpage for my client. I selected Sara, a cooking enthusiast from Brooklyn, who enjoys serving her Trinidadian delicacies such as doubles to others. By working on this project, I was able to learn about the design thinking process.

I conducted an interview with my client to identify her requirements, favorite colors (red and gold), and the tone she wanted for her webpage (playful and inviting). The main learning from this interview is that listening is essential for designing a user-centered webpage.

The next step was creating a define statement: “Sara requires a platform to communicate her authentic Trinidadian recipe and family cooking story in order to document her culture and reach out to people who love Caribbean cuisine.”

Following that, I used my imagination by creating an idea board for ideas collection. I found color schemes, fonts, family, and Trinidad’s national flag.

Finally, I coded the web page using HTML with semantic tags like <strong>, <em>, and <mark> to make it accessible for screen readers. I added content about Sara's cooking contest win, her favorite dish (doubles), and her cultural background.

Overall, this project taught me how to move from empathy to definition to ideation to prototyping. I learned that good web design is not just about code - it's about solving real problems for real people.
